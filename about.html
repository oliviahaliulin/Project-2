THE STORY OF DRAKE

Aubrey Drake Graham was born in Toronto on October 24, 1986. Raised between his mother’s home in Canada and his father’s musical roots in Memphis, he grew up surrounded by rhythm and storytelling. Before music, Drake first gained fame as Jimmy Brooks on Degrassi: The Next Generation. While acting, he was already writing and recording songs in his basement.

In 2006, he released his first mixtape Room for Improvement, followed by Comeback Season. His breakthrough came with So Far Gone in 2009 — featuring “Best I Ever Had” and “Successful” — which caught Lil Wayne’s attention and led to a deal with Young Money Entertainment. His debut album Thank Me Later topped the charts in 2010, marking the start of his evolution into one of the world’s biggest artists.

Through albums like Take Care, Nothing Was the Same, and Views, Drake redefined hip-hop and R&B by blending raw emotion with mainstream appeal. Beyond music, he built his own label and lifestyle brand, October’s Very Own (OVO), turning Toronto into a cultural symbol. From TV star to worldwide icon, Drake’s story is one of ambition, emotion, and reinvention.

MEET DRAKE

Meet Aubrey Drake Graham — the Toronto-born artist who transformed hip-hop, R&B, and pop into something entirely his own. Before becoming one of the most streamed musicians in the world, Drake’s storytelling talent shone as an actor on Degrassi: The Next Generation.

In 2009, his breakthrough mixtape So Far Gone changed everything, blending vulnerability and confidence in a way no one else had done before. That moment launched a career filled with chart-topping albums like Take Care, Views, and Certified Lover Boy.

Drake isn’t just a musician — he’s a brand, a movement, and a voice that connects across generations. Through his label and lifestyle brand, October’s Very Own (OVO), he’s built a creative empire rooted in authenticity and innovation. From his Toronto roots to global fame, Drake’s journey proves that emotion, ambition, and art can rewrite the rules of music.
